Despite the impressive performance, its practical deployment is constrained by computational complexity and redundancy in the sequential denoising process. While feature caching across timesteps has proven effective in accelerating diffusion models, its application to DiT is limited by fundamental architectural differences from U-Net-based approaches. Through empirical analysis of DiT feature dynamics, we identify that significant feature variation between DiT blocks presents a key challenge for feature reusability. To address this, we convert standard DiT into Skip-DiT with skip branches to enhance feature smoothness. Further, we introduce Skip-Cache which utilizes the skip branches to cache DiT features across timesteps at the inference time. We validated effectiveness of our proposal on different DiT backbones for video and image generation, showcasing skip branches to help preserve generation quality and achieve higher speedup. Experimental results indicate that Skip-DiT achieves a 1.5 × 1.5\\times 1.5 × speedup almost for free and a 2.2 × 2.2\\times 2.2 × speedup with only a minor reduction in quantitative metrics. Report issue for preceding element\n\n1 Introduction\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nDiffusion models [ \n\n9\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib9\n\n, \n\n3\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib3\n\n, \n\n24\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib24\n\n, \n\n46\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib46\n\n] have emerged as the de-facto solution for visual generation, owing to their high fidelity outputs and ability to incorporate various conditioning signals, particularly natural language. Classical diffusion models, which adopt U-Net [ \n\n27\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib27\n\n] as their denoising backbone, have dominated image and video generation applications. More recently, Diffusion Transformers (DiT) [ \n\n4\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib4\n\n, \n\n23\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib23\n\n] have introduced an alternative architecture that replaces traditional sequential convolutional networks with Vision Transformers, offering enhanced scalability potential. While initially designed for image generation, DiT has demonstrated remarkable effectiveness when extended to video generation tasks [ \n\n19\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib19\n\n, \n\n16\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib16\n\n, \n\n25\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib25\n\n] . However, despite these advances, significant challenges remain in scaling diffusion models efficiently, particularly for applications involving large numbers of input tokens such as video generation. This scaling challenge is especially pronounced in DiT architectures, where the attention mechanism's computational complexity grows quadratically with input size. The magnitude of this challenge is illustrated by Sora [ \n\n20\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib20\n\n] , a state-of-the-art video generation model that remains unavailable to the public.\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nNumerous approaches have been proposed to enhance the efficiency of diffusion models, including reduced sampling techniques [ \n\n33\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib33\n\n] , distillation methods [ \n\n41\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib41\n\n, \n\n29\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib29\n\n] , and quantization strategies [ \n\n5\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib5\n\n] . Caching mechanisms, which reuse noise latents across timesteps, have emerged as a particularly promising direction as they do not require extensive model retraining [ \n\n18\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib18\n\n, \n\n15\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib15\n\n, \n\n38\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib38\n\n, \n\n47\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib47\n\n, \n\n6\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib6\n\n] . However, many existing caching approaches [ \n\n18\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib18\n\n, \n\n38\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib38\n\n] are specifically tailored to U-Net architectures, leveraging their unique structural properties—particularly the skip connections between paired downsampling and upsampling blocks that enable high-level feature reuse while selectively updating low-level features. While some recent studies [ \n\n47\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib47\n\n, \n\n6\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib6\n\n] have attempted to adapt caching mechanisms for DiT acceleration, they have not achieved the same level of efficiency gains and performance preservation as their U-Net counterparts.\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\n \n\n(a) Denoising step = 10 Report issue for preceding element\n\n \n\n(b) Denoising step = 250 Report issue for preceding element\n\nFigure 2: Feature smoothness analysis of DiT with class-to-video generation on Taichi using DDPM. Report issue for preceding element\n\nTo understand the key challenges of feature caching in DiT, we analyze the feature dynamics during the denoising process. Drawing inspiration from loss landscape visualization techniques [ \n\n13\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib13\n\n, \n\n11\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib11\n\n] , we examine feature changes using the early and late timesteps in denoising as a case study. For effective caching, features should exhibit minimal variation between timesteps, allowing us to reuse features from previous steps and bypass computation in subsequent Transformer blocks. We term this property ”feature smoothness”, which manifests as flatness in the landscape visualization. However, as illustrated in Figure \n\n2\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#S1.F2\n\n, we observe that vanilla DiT (w/o skip branches) exhibits high feature variance across timesteps, contrary to the desired characteristics for effective caching. Thus, we ask:\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\n(Q) What are the new insights can we gain from feature smoothness in DiT architectures, and how can these insights inform the design of more effective inference caching mechanisms? Report issue for preceding element\n\nDrawing from the findings in [ \n\n13\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib13\n\n] , where residual connections are shown capable of mitigating sharp loss landscapes, in this study, ❶ we first conduct preliminary experiments adding skip branches to pre-trained DiT model from shallow to deep blocks, named Skip-DiT . which achieves significantly improved feature smoothness with minimal continuous pre-training, as demonstrated in Figure \n\n2\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#S1.F2\n\n (w/ skip branches). ❷ We then leverage these skip branches during inference to implement an efficient caching mechanism, Skip-Cache , where only the first DiT block's output needs to be computed for subsequent timesteps while deep block outputs are cached and reused. ❸ To evaluate our proposal, we conduct extensive experiments across multiple DiT backbones, covering image and video generation, class-conditioned and text-conditioned generation. We demonstrate that Skip-DiT consistently outperforms both dense baselines and existing caching mechanisms in both qualitative and quantitative evaluations. Our contributions are three-fold:\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\n• We identify feature smoothness as a critical factor limiting the effectiveness of cross-timestep feature caching in DiT, which helps better understand caching efficiency. Report issue for preceding element\n\n• We build Skip-DiT , a skip-branch augmented DiT architecture that enhances feature smoothness, and Skip-Cache that efficiently leveraging skip branches for feature caching across timesteps. Skip-Cache facilitates accelerated inference via caching while maintaining visual generation performance. Report issue for preceding element\n\n• Extensive empirical evaluations demonstrate that Skip-Cache achieves substantial acceleration: up to 1.5 × 1.5\\times 1.5 × speedup is achieved almost for free and a 2.2 × 2.2\\times 2.2 × speedup with only a minor reduction in quantitative metrics. Report issue for preceding element\n\n2 Related Works\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nTransformer-based Diffusion Models\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nDiffusion model has become the dominating architecture for image and video generation, whose main idea is iterative generate high-fidelity images or video frames from noise [ \n\n26\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib26\n\n] . Early diffusion models mainly employ U-Net as their denoising backbone [ \n\n24\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib24\n\n, \n\n3\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib3\n\n] . However, U-Net architectures struggle to model long-range dependencies due to the local nature of convolutions. Researchers proposing diffusion transformer model (DiT) for image generation [ \n\n4\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib4\n\n, \n\n2\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib2\n\n, \n\n23\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib23\n\n] . Recent years have witnessed a significant growth in studies of video DiT. Proprietary DiT such as Sora [ \n\n20\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib20\n\n] and Movie-Gen [ \n\n25\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib25\n\n] show impressive generation quality, also evidenced by open-sourced implementation [ \n\n48\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib48\n\n, \n\n12\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib12\n\n] . Latte decomposes the spatial and temporal dimensions into four efficient variants for handling video tokens, allowing effective modeling of the substantial number of tokens extracted from videos in the latent space [ \n\n19\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib19\n\n] . CogvideoX adds a 3D VAE combined with an expert transformer using adaptive LayerNorm, which enables the generation of longer, high-resolution videos [ \n\n40\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib40\n\n] . However, as the number of tokens grows exponentially with video length and spatial resolution, the computational complexity of DiT especially the self-attention mechanism remains a significant bottleneck for video generation.\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nDiffusion Acceleration with Feature Caching\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nSince Diffusion model involves iterative denoising, caching features across time-steps, model layers, and modules has been found an effective way to save inference computation costs. For U-Net Diffusion, DeepCache [ \n\n18\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib18\n\n] and FRDiff [ \n\n32\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib32\n\n] exploit temporal redundancy by reusing features across adjacent denoising steps. While other works take a more structured approach by analyzing and caching specific architectural components–Faster Diffusion [ \n\n15\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib15\n\n] specifically targets encoder feature reuse while enabling parallel decoder computation, and Block Caching [ \n\n38\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib38\n\n] introduces automated caching schedules for different network blocks based on their temporal stability patterns. Recently, cache-based acceleration has also been applied to DiT. PAB [ \n\n47\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib47\n\n] introduces a pyramid broadcasting strategy for attention outputs. Δ Δ \\Delta roman_Δ -DiT [ \n\n6\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib6\n\n] proposes adaptive caching of different DiT blocks based on their roles in a generation–rear blocks during early sampling for details and front blocks during later stages for outlines. T-Gate [ \n\n45\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib45\n\n] identifies a natural two-stage inference process, enabling the caching and reuse of text-oriented semantic features after the initial semantics-planning stage. While these caching techniques have shown promise, they are primarily limited to inference-time optimization, and there remains significant potential for improving their acceleration factors.\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\n3 Methodology\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\n \n\nFigure 3: Illustration of Skip-DiT and Skip-Cache for DiT visual generation caching. \n\n19\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib19\n\n] , as our base model. Hunyuan-DiT [ \n\n16\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib16\n\n] , the first text-to-image DiT model with skip branches, modifies the model structure by splitting transformer blocks into encoder and decoder blocks, which are connected via long skip connections, similar to UNets. In this work, we leverage Hunyuan-DiT to investigate the effectiveness of skip connections in text-to-image tasks. Additionally, we modify the structure of Latte, following the guidance in Figure \n\n3\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#S3.F3\n\n, to evaluate the performance of skip connections in video generation tasks and explore techniques for integrating skip connections into a pre-trained DiT model.\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nDatasets\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\nIn the class-to-video task, we conduct comprehensive experiments on four public datasets: FaceForensics [ \n\n28\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib28\n\n] , SkyTimelapse [ \n\n39\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib39\n\n] , UCF101 [ \n\n34\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib34\n\n] , and Taichi-HD [ \n\n31\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib31\n\n] . During training, we update all parameters in Skip-DiT, including skip branches. For text-to-video tasks, we propose a two-stage continual training strategy as follows:\n\nReport issue for preceding element\n\n• Skip-branch training: The models are initialized with the weights of the original text-to-video Latte model, while the weight of skip branches are initialized randomly. During this stage, we train only the skip branches until the model can roughly generate items. This stage takes approximately one day. Report issue for preceding element\n\n• Overall training: After fully training the skip branches, we unfreeze all other parameters and perform overall training. At this stage, Skip-DiT rapidly recovers its generation capability within approximately two days and can generate content comparable to the original Latte with an additional three days of training. Report issue for preceding element\n\nThis strategy significantly reduces training costs compared to training from scratch. All our training experiments are conducted on 8 H100 GPUs, employing the video-image joint training strategy proposed in Ma et al. [ \n\n19\n\nhttps://arxiv.org/html/2411.17616v1#bib.bib19\n\n] . Skip-Cache shares significant similarities with DeepCache but extends the method to DiT models. Specifically, we upgrade traditional DiT models to Skip-DiT and cache them using Skip-Cache .
